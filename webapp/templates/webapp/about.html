About This Dashboard

What is this tool?

This is an independent, community-driven tracker for U.S. immigration priority dates published in the monthly Visa Bulletin by the Department of State.

The dashboard helps family-sponsored and employment-based visa applicants:

  • Track historical priority date movements over time
  • See when their priority date might become current
  • Understand retrogression patterns and trends
  • Compare movement across countries and categories

Where does the data come from?

All data is sourced directly from the official U.S. Department of State Visa Bulletin archives.

The dashboard automatically fetches new bulletins daily and parses cutoff dates going back to 2015. No manual data entry is involved—everything is extracted programmatically.

How do the projections work?

Projections are calculated using a simple rolling average:

  1. We look at the last 12 months of cutoff date movements.
  2. We calculate the average number of days the cutoff moves forward per month.
  3. We project forward from the most recent bulletin to estimate when your priority date might become current.

Important caveats:

  • These are rough estimates, not guarantees.
  • Visa Bulletin movements are influenced by many unpredictable factors: policy changes, application surges, Congressional action, USCIS processing speeds, etc.
  • Retrogressions (backward movements) can happen suddenly.
  • Use projections as a rough guide, not an official timeline.

Who built this?

My name is Vladimir Yakunin. I was sick of the siloed data scattered across different government pages and wanted to understand when my brother's immigration case would actually get processed. So I built this dashboard to visualize the trends and make sense of the Visa Bulletin's monthly updates.

If you're in the same boat—trying to figure out when your (or a loved one's) priority date will become current—I hope this tool helps you too.

The project is open source and available on GitHub. Contributions, bug reports, and feature requests are welcome!

Is this affiliated with USCIS or the State Department?

No. This is an independent, volunteer project with no affiliation to any government agency.

For official information, always refer to:

  • U.S. Department of State Visa Bulletin
  • USCIS.gov
  • Your immigration attorney
